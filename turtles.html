SEA

Turtles

There are seven species of sea turtles all ranging in different sizes and shapes. Although their vision is better underwater, they prefer to be close to the land. Females come to the shore when it's time to lay their eggs and just like other reptiles, the sex of sea turtles depend on the temperature of their nest (sand). Baby turtles will hatch in about 60 days where they find their way to the ocean through cues such as light from the ocean horizon or the slope of the beach.

Threats

Six out of the seven species of sea turtles are endangered. Thousands of turtle hatchlings are born each year along the Southeast Coast of the U.S. but only 1 in 1,000 to 10,000 will survive and become adults. Amongst natural obstacles, sea turtles are endangered primarily because of human threats such as poaching and habitat destruction.

Poachers

Thousands of sea turtles are slaughtered for their eggs, meat, skin, and shells in return for income. Bycatch (accidental capture) is also a top cause for deaths of sea turtles in the U.S., amounting to about 4,600 deaths a year.

Habitat Destruction

Human activity and development in beaches disrupt turtle nesting and hatchlings. Coral reefs, which are the main feeding ground, also gets destroyed through activities like sedimentation.

Climate Change and Pollution

The rising sea levels and temperature also causes damage to nesting and foraging grounds. Plastic bags are mistaken for jelly fish and gets consumed by sea turtles which eventually kills them.

What We Can Do

Raise Awareness

Support organizations like the World Wildlife Fund that raise awareness and help communities develop opportunities where they do not need to depend on turtle harvesting for income.

Turtle-Friendly Choices

Support turtle-friendly tourism by looking for vacation establishments that are proactive about protecting turtle nests on their beaches.

Limit Sea Pollution

Recycle and reduce single-use plastic when you can by refusing unneeded single-use plastic or bringing reusable versions of the product, such as grocery bags, coffee cups, straws, etc.
