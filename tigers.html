SUNDA TIGERS

SUNDA

Tigers

Once found throughout several parts of the Sunda Islands of Indonesia, Sunda tigers are now only found in Sumatra. The Sunda tiger is a subspecies of the Panthera tigris and is known for their heavy black stripes, with each pattern being completely unique to that tiger. They commonly have beards or manes around their faces and are the smallest tiger subspecies.

Status

Critically Endangered

Population

Around 400

Habitat

Forests, wetlands, grasslands

Height

Up to 8 feet

Weight

260 pounds

Poached

About 55 a year

Threats

The Sunda tigers are estimated to be declining at a rate of 3.2% - 5.9% a year. Most Sunda tigers only roam within the protected conservation areas or national parks, but with looming threats such as poaching and habitat loss - the Sunda tiger population continues to decline.

Poachers

The top threat and cause of 80% of Sunda tiger deaths. Tigers are poached for their skin, bones, canines, and claws. Researchers observed about 20% of people entering the protected conservation zones illegaly are armed poachers.

Habitat Loss

Forests are cleared due to teh expansion of coffee, palm oil, and urban development. Deforestation seperates tiger population which limits breeding and feeding.

Human-Wildlife Conflict

Sunda tigers explore human areas due to habitat destruction and lack of food. They end up being killed in retaliation when they end up eating farmer's livestock.

What We Can Do

Buy Forest-Friendly Products

Avoid products containing palm oil or wood that has been unsustainably harvested and look for forest friendly items.

Protect Habitats

Support organizations like the International Union for the Conservation of Nature (IUCN) that are dedicated to preserving endangered species.

Reduce Demand

Diminish the demand for tiger products which is the main drive and motive for poachers.
