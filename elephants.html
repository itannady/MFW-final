AFRICAN SAVANNA

Elephants

Known as the largest land mammal in the world, the African Savanna Elephants can grow up to 13 feet tall and weight 14,000lbs. Herds of elephants usually consist of females with their calves and is led by the eldest female, known as the matriarch. Male elephants lead a solitary life and will approach a female if they need to mate. The African Savanna Elephants help plants and animals thrive by maintaining savannas and open woodlands by moving around and feeding – reducing tree densities and dispersing seeds.

Threats

Over the last 50 years, at least 60% of the African Savanna Elephant population has decreased. Due to poaching, habitat destruction, and climate change - only 415,000 African Savanna elephants remain across Africa.

Poachers

Thousands of elephants are killed each year for ivory - viewed as a status symbol and used to produce ornaments, jewelry, piano keys, and more.

Habitat Destruction

Elephants require a large amount of food and area. With the increase of lands being stripped apart for commercial and agricultural use, elephants have limited resources. This also grants poachers access to elephants easily.

Climate Change

Less rain is causing water to be a rare resource in elephant habitats. Elephants need at least 250 liters a day. The rising temperatures is a threat as elephants struggle to find water.

What We Can Do

Reduce Demand

New ivory is currently banned, but old antique ivory still circulates in the market. Show solidarity with the elephants by not purchasing ivory.

Elephant Friendly Products

Some corporations in the coffee/tea industry take over elephant habitats. Look for certified fair-trade coffee that is not made from elephant habitats.

Spread the Word

Support conservation efforts through organizations such as the World Wildlife Fund.
