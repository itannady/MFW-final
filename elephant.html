Elephants

Known as the largest land mammal in the world, the African Savanna Elephants can grow up to 13 feet tall and weight 14,000lbs. Herds of elephants usually consist of females with their calves and is led by the eldest female, known as the matriarch. Male elephants lead a solitary life and will approach a female if they need to mate. The African Savanna Elephants help plants and animals thrive by maintaining savannas and open woodlands by moving around and feeding – reducing tree densities and dispersing seeds.

Threats

Over the last 50 years, at least 60% of the African Savanna Elephant population has decreased. Due to poaching, habitat destruction, and climate change - only 415,000 African Savanna elephants remain across Africa.
